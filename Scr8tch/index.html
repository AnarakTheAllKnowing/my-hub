🦾 Elbow-Safe

SCR8TCH
MODIFICATIONS

All movements that load the elbow under sustained tension, deep flexion, or bodyweight hanging have been replaced.

Removed Exercise Replaced With Rationale
Pull-ups / Chin-upsLat Pulldown (various grips)Eliminates bodyweight hanging and elbow extension under load
Ring Rows / TRX Rows / Inverted RowsChest-Supported DB Row / Cable RowRemoves sustained grip and wrist pronation stress
Dips (all variations)DB Floor Press / Cable Tricep ExtensionAvoids deep elbow flexion under bodyweight load
Barbell / Hammer / Reverse CurlsCable Rope Curl (light, controlled)Constant cable tension is gentler than free weight peak loading
Hanging Leg Raises / Toes to BarLying Leg Raise (flat or weighted)Eliminates dead hang grip entirely
Renegade RowsHalf-Kneeling Cable RowRemoves plank-position grip stress on wrist/elbow
Barbell Hang CleansDB Hang Clean (neutral grip)Neutral grip reduces forearm torsion and elbow shear
Lat Hang (cooldown stretch)Overhead Band Lat StretchSame lat lengthening without hanging bodyweight
Ring/TRX Push-UpsDB Floor PressEliminates unstable grip demand on elbow joint
Daily Elbow Prehab Protocol — Before every session + rest days
1
Wrist Stretches
30 sec each direction, both arms
Straighten arm, pull fingers back (extensors) then down (flexors)
2
Eccentric Wrist Curls
15 slow negatives (3 sec down)
3–5 lb DB, both flexion and extension
3
Band Pronation/Supination
15 reps each direction
Elbow pinned to side, rotate palm up and down
4
Soft Tissue Work
60 sec per side
Lacrosse ball on flexor/extensor origins below elbow crease
5
Ice (if needed)
10 min post-training
Only if discomfort present during session
Re-Introduction Test Once you can hold a 30-second dead hang with zero pain for 3 consecutive sessions, begin reintroducing hanging movements at reduced volume (2 sets of 5). Increase by 1 set per week if pain-free.
